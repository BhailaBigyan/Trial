Accurate measurement

A given measurementis said to be accurate in reaktion to the other similar measurements. If the error involve in it is least.


Precise measurement

A given measurementis said to be precise in realtion to other similar measurements. If it is taken with an instrunment with minimun least count.


Significant figure

The number of reliable digits in the measuewmwnt of physical quanitites is called the significant figure.


Rules for determining the significant figure

  1. Number with no decimal point
    1. All the non zero digits are significant.
    2. All zeros between the non-zero digits are significant.
    3. Zero at the beginning and the end of the number are not significant.
  2. Number with decimal point
    1. All zero and nonzero digits to the left and right of decimal oint are significant.
    2. All zero and non-zero digits to the right of decimal point are significant.
    3. The zeros after the decimal point are non significant. If there is no non-zero digit before the3 decimal point

Four catagories of physical quantities

The physical quanitiy for variable and consatnts may be dimensional or dimesnsionless.

  1. Dimesnsional Variable

    These are the physical quantities which have dimensions but donot have a constant value. For eg; velocity, displacement, acceleration, force energy, impulse, etc.

  2. Dimesnsional Constant

    These are the physical quantities which have dimensions and have constant vlaue. For eg; acceleration due to gravity, universal gravitational constant, velocity of light, etc.

  3. Dimesnsionless Variable

    These are the physical quantities which have no dimesions and donot have constant value. For eg; strain, relative density, solid angle, corfficient of friction, etc

  4. Dimesnsionless Constant

    These are the physical quantities which have no dimensions and but have constant value. For eg; vlaue of e, natural number, etc.


Limitations of Dimensional Analysis

  1. It supplies no information about dimesnsionlesss constant. They have to be determine either by the experiment or by the mathematical investigation.
  2. This method is used only in the case of power function. It fails in the case of exponential function, logarithmic function, trigonometric function.
  3. This method fails to derice directly a relation which contains two or more than two quantites of like nature
  4. It fails to derive exact form of physical relation in which a physical quantity depends upon more than three other physical quantities. This is because powert of M,K & T, we can obtain only three equations. Three equations cannot determine more than unknown quantities.
  5. If we cannot identify all the factors on which the physical quantity depends, then the method of dimensional analysis cannot be used to derive the expression for a physical quantity.
  6. It can only check weather a physical relation is dimensionally correct or not. It cannot tell wether the relation is absolutly correct or not.

Vector

Vector quantity

It is defined as the physical quanity which has magnitude as well as direction. For example: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, impulse, moment, momentum, weight, electric field intensity, magnitetic field intensity, etc.


Scaler quantity

It is defined as the physical quantity which has magnitude onl but not direction. For example: distance, time, charge, speed, pressure, area, density, volume, work, energy, power, etc.


Difference between vector ans scaler quantity

Graphically a vector is represented by a straight line with an arrow head.
